3. Data Collection on Our Website

Server Log Files

The provider of the pages automatically collects and stores information in so-called server log files, which your browser automatically transmits to us. These are:

  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system used
  • Referrer URL
  • Hostname of the accessing computer
  • Time of the server request
  • IP address

This data is not merged with other data sources. The collection of this data is based on Art. 6 Para. 1 lit. f GDPR.

4. Analysis Tools (Tracking)

We use anonymized statistics to understand the use of our website. No personal profiles are created. You can object to the collection at any time via our Opt-Out page.

5. Lux Client (Launcher)

The Lux Client Launcher communicates with external APIs to load versions, mods, and skins:

  • Mojang/Microsoft: For authentication and downloading Minecraft game files.
  • Modrinth: For searching and downloading mods (Modpack Editor).
  • Crafatar: For displaying player skins/heads.

When using these services, the respective privacy policies of the providers apply. The launcher itself does not store passwords; authentication occurs via secure OAuth procedures from Microsoft.

6. Your Rights

Under the applicable legal provisions, you have the following rights at any time:

  • Access (Art. 15 GDPR): You have the right to receive information about your stored personal data free of charge.
  • Rectification (Art. 16 GDPR): You can request the correction of incorrect data.
  • Erasure (Art. 17 GDPR): You have the right to request the deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten").
  • Restriction (Art. 18 GDPR): You can request the restriction of processing.
  • Data Portability (Art. 20 GDPR): You have the right to have your data handed over in a common format.
  • Objection (Art. 21 GDPR): You can object to data processing.

To exercise these rights, please contact the address provided above.

7. Right to Lodge a Complaint with a Supervisory Authority

In the event of data protection violations, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority.
